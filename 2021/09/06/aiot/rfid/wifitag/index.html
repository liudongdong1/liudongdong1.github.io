Gao, C., Li, Y., & Zhang, X. (2018). LiveTag: Sensing human-object interaction through passive chipless WiFi tags. In 15th {USENIX} Symposium on Networked Systems Design and Implementation ({NSDI} 18) (pp. 533-546). level: CCF_A; NSDI

Paper: LiveTag


LiveTag: Sensing human-object interaction through passive chipless WiFi tags.

Summary

  1. touched by fingers, it distuibs ambient WiFi channel in a deterministic way, multiple metallic structures can be printed on the same tag to create unique touch points.
  2. LiveTag incorporates customized multi-antenna beamforming algorithms that allow WiFi receivers to sense the tag and discriminate the touch events, amid multipath reflections/interferences.
  3. the tag ids designed as a strong reflector for 2.4/5 GHz signals from a cooperating WiFi transmitter, and the touches upon its metal structure create a known non-linear channel distortion which can be detected by a WiFi receiver.

Research Objective

  • Application Area: HCI
    • Purpose: detect manipulation with physical objects

Proble Statement

  • LOS channel between the WiFi transmitter and receiver is much stronger and can easily overhelm the signals reflected by the tag;
  • due to frequency-selective fading caused by ambient multipath reflections, the spectrum signatures tend to be interfered by random channel gain variations across the frequency band.
  • to enable robust detection, we design a fading suppression and LOS nulling mecanism, taking advantage of the multiple antennas on the WiFi transceivers.

previous work:

  • sensing interaction with objects:
    • vision based: practical systems have proved difficult to engineer, especially under unknown background, moving scenes, and challenging light conditions, high computational cost. Barcode
    • activate sensors based: carry circuit components and need battery maintenance, unsuitable for scaling to a large number of low-value objects. RFID(expensive, dedicated reader, cann’t different touch ppositions on one tag)
  • Chipless RFID tags: Chipless tags encode information either in time or frequency domain, time-domain use multiple RF circulators to induce different delays to passing signals, thus creating signatures; Frequency-domainL use multiple RF filters on the tag’s frequency response to create signatures. costly high UWB tags reaches sub-meter ragne due to the FCC’s transmission power regulation. LiveTag is responsive to touch, rather than increasing the tag capacity.

Methods

one antenna receive the interogation signal and passed through a transmission ilne and filtered by a multi-resonator network. Each antenna simultaneously receives interrogating signals and propagated them towards the opposite direction. Maximize the change of spectrum upon touch, by optimizing each resonator's filter gain, defined as the ratio between incidental and emitting signal strength.

1. Resonator Model （谐振器设计）

等效电路，影响因素，需要实现那几个指标，具体谐振器设计(从一下几个方面)，

  • material of substrate and conductive layer
  • impact of the resonator’s geometrical parameters
    • resonator size
    • gap between resonator and transmission line
  • 2D bandstop filer: 2D 带阻滤波。
  • at resonance frequency, microwaves form standing waves in the resonator , oscillating with large amplitudes, thus confining the energy within the resonator. 共振频率下，微波在谐振器中形成驻波，引起大的震荡，从而锁定谐振器中的能量。
  • the resonator can be modeled by an equivalent circuit, comprised of a cascade of capacitor $C_r$​, inductor $L_r$, and resistor $R_r$​​​​​​, whose values are determined by the resonator’s material and geometry. 谐振器可以是由等效电路建模（图4），包括电容器Cr、电感器Lr和电阻器Rr的级联，其值由谐振器的材料决定和几何学

  • three requiments:
    • a resonator’s frequency response should not be affected by other adjacent ones.
    • the filter bnadwidth of the resonator must be narrow, so to pack multiple stopbands into the limited WiFi spectrum.
    • the filter gain at resonance frequncy should be large, allowing the tag presene and touch events to be easily detectable.
    • to support multi-touch, multiple resonators must be co-located, but with no mutual coupling.
  • the interference between planar resonators becomes negligible if they are coupled to a common transmission line in a non-contact manner(如果平面谐振器以非接触方式耦合到公共传输线，则平面谐振器之间的干扰可以忽略不计)
  • (the substrate, meterial of the conductive layer, the resonator’s geometry)
    • the thickness and dielectric constant of substrate affect the center frequency of the stopbands.

2. resonator responses to touch

model human body as RC network consisting of resistors, capacitors. A finger touching the resonator can be regarded as adding a capacitor and a resistor in parallel to the original resonator’s components. Leads to an equivalent increase of$C_r$​, thus making the notch's center frequency disappear from its original position

Multiple resonators: creating spectrum signatures and enhancing filter gain: 将多个相同的谐振器放置在相同的位置共振频率彼此接近，形成复合触点。

Coupling between resonators: only in the nearfield when the resonators are placed in close proximity. When the separation similar exceeds 5mm, the drifting effect becomes negligible.

3. Extracting frequency response of a Tag

creating artificial fading with orthogonal beamforming. From the perspective of the tag, although the beams may coe from different angles, the resonator will cause the same notch position on the CSI.

smoothing CSI with PCA, and preserving the 2nd and 3rd components.

simultaneously suppressing the LOS channel

Tag Presence detection and Identification: by comparing the measured CSI with ground-truth spectrum signature of each deployed tag.

Identify a touch event, by tracking the change across two consecutive snapshots of the frequency response. LiveTag uses multiple co-located resonators with different frequency notches to represent one compound touch point.

Conclusion

  • Tag design, the first system to design touch-sensitive, WiFi-detectable tags that can sense human-object interaction.
  • Tag detection, design new beamforming mechanisms to suppress ambient multipath and LOS interference, enabling a pair of WiFi Tx/Rx to detect the passive tag ad multipoint touch events in practival environment.
  • implement the tags using stardard PCB printing technique, verify the feasibility and accuracy.

Notes

1. 谐振器

谐振器就是指产生谐振频率的电子元件，常用的分为石英晶体谐振器和陶瓷谐振器。产生频率的作用，具有稳定，抗干扰性能良好的特点，广泛应用于各种电子产品中。石英晶体谐振器的频率精度要高于陶瓷谐振器，但成本也比陶瓷谐振器高。谐振器主要起频率控制的作用，所有电子产品涉及频率的发射和接收都需要谐振器。谐振器的类型按照外形可以分为直插和贴片式两种。

  • 金属波导：传输电磁能量或电磁信号的途径可分为两类，一类是电磁波在空间或大气中的传播，另一类是电磁波沿波导系统的传播。双线传输线主要用在频率较低的场合，当使用频率逐步提高时，双线传输线的传输损耗以及辐射损耗急剧的增加，为了克服辐射损耗，采用了同轴线结构。但是同轴线中所采用的模式仍然是TEM 模，必须有内外两根导体，到了频率更高时内导体的损耗变得很严重。在微波频段即分米波段和厘米波段人们发现，用一根中空的金属管来传输电磁波是可行的和方便的。在空管中不可能传播 TEM模式，因此采用 TE 模或 TM 模，这就是金属波导或称为波导管。到了短毫米波段及亚微毫米波段金属波导的截面积尺寸太小，加工不易，因此采用介质波导作为传输系统。在光波段使用光学纤维和光波导也是介质波导。 光学纤维简称光纤已成为传输电磁信号的主要手段。
2. 震荡器

振荡器是将直流电能转变成交流电能的过程，用来产生一定频率的交流信号，是有源器件。谐振器是电路对一定频率的信号进行谐振，主要是用来筛选出某一频率，是无源器件。晶体谐振器是无源器件，不需要电源。晶体振荡器是有源器件，需要电源，且晶体振荡器的电路中最重要的元件就是晶体谐振器。将晶体谐振器加外部振荡电路、放大电路、滤波电路等等即为晶体振荡器。
